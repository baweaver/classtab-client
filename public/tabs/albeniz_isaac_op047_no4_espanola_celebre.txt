CELEBRE SERENATA ESPANOLA (CADIZ) - Isaac Albeniz

This is a beautiful but tricky piece.
It contains a lot of 16th note triplets and reaches up to the 17th fret.
For those folk who are scared to venture beyond the 12th position,
here is a chance to conquer that phobia!
The basic rhythm is 1-123-2-and-3-and ect...
This transcription is based on the guitar arangement of Miguel Llobet.

E-mail me if there are any mistakes so I can fix them.
email: dkahts@tsamail.trsa.ac.za [no longer active]

Tablature explanation at the end of the piece.

CADIZ - Isaac Albeniz

Key signature: D major and D minor
Tuning: DADGBE
Time signature: 3/4
Tempo: Allegretto ma non troppo (moderately)

 Isaac Albeniz - Suite Espanola Op. 47 n. 4 - Cadiz

 Tabbed by: The_S

 The_S: I first heard this played by John Williams, on a cd called Echoes of
        Spain. From the moment it started, I was already nodding my head to
        the beat. Unfortunately, the only sheet music I found for it was from
        the Icelandic Guitar School, and had been simplified for student use.
        I did however come across a nice, accurate Midi of the piano version
        (original) which is more complex than the guitar version played by
        John Williams. So I converted the Midi to tab, and reworked it to suit
        the fingers of a guitarist. I also referred to the Icelandic sheet
        music for a nice comprimise between the two versions. The result is
        this tab, which is slightly more complex than the version John Williams
        plays, but still very managable. A few notes may be off, so feel free
        to make corrections.

 Tuning: EADGBE (Standard)
